Things you need to know when renting in London


Navigating renting in London can be pretty overwhelming.

So we've given you some pointers about where to start.

Hover over the boxes to get some tips!


Where to look for a place


There are many places to start looking for your new London home

Why not try:


  • SpareRoom is a good place to look for spare rooms across London. It's free to use, but some for features you need to be a subscriber. This is a good place to look if you are looking for short-term lets. As with all free sites, make sure you view the place before you hand over any money.

  • OpenRent links landlords and tenants directly which reduces fees!

  • rentsquare is another way to find places with lower rent and lower fees.

  • RightMove is also a great site for longer term rentals, although the listings tend to be from agents who may charge high fees.

  • PrimeMove is similar to RightMove, it has lots of longer term listings by agents across London.

  • You might also like to try Gumtree or websites (or shop windows) of letting agents. Hover over the Letting Agents box for a few pointers!

Types of rental properties


There are lots of different rental options. These things will help you decide what you're looking for


  • Short term or long term? Lets less than 12 months are usually called short term lets. If you're looking for a short term let, make sure you don't waste your time looking at places that are only available for longer.

  • Whole place or flatshare? You could rent a whole place and divide the rent with some friends or do a flatshare. A flatshare is when you rent a bedroom and share the rest of the house with other flatsharers.

  • Furnished or unfurnished? You might save money by renting an unfurnished place, but if you have to buy furniture it might cost more!

  • Through a letting agent or directly with the landlord?You don't have to go through a letting agent. Many landlords prefer to rent directly (try OpenRent or SpareRoom).


Letting Agents


There are millions of letting agents in London. Some are better than others, so how do you know who to use?

Here are some things to think about:


  • Letting agents can be helpful as they can ensure your tenancy is lawful and your bond is secure. They can act between you and the landlord and they might be able to show you multiple properties at more convenient times.

  • The larger ones (such as Foxtons, Black Katz) probably charge higher fees but are more like to have smooth procedures

  • Remember to always ask about all the fees associated with the tenancy including agent fees, reference fees, application fees, lease termination fees, tenancy change fees, early check out fees, inventory fees, late fees and any other fees!

Check out allAgents for reviews of letting agents


Things to ask when you view a place


Make sure you make the most of your viewings by asking these things:


  • The amount of rent (weekly or monthly)

  • The amount of deposit

  • All fees associated with the property

  • Council tax

  • Approximate cost of bills per month

  • Are any bills included in the rent?

  • How to make an application

  • Why are the previous tenants moving?

  • What date is it available?

  • How long is the tenancy?

  • What are the other tenants like?

  • Where's the nearest tube station/bus stop?

  • Where's the nearest supermarket?



Things to check when you view a place


Make sure you make the most of your viewings by checking these things:


  • Do the windows close properly?

  • Is there enough storage?

  • Is there any damp/mould?

  • How noisy is it? Look for bars, trains and busy roads close by.

  • Is the area safe and is the place secure?

  • Do the appliances work?

  • Does it come furnished?

  • Is it very cold or warm?

  • Water pressure

  • Are there any signs of vermin?
