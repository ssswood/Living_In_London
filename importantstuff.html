Things you need to know when renting in London


Navigating renting in London can be pretty overwhelming.

Here are some pointers about where to start.




Types of rental properties

  • Short term or long term? If you're looking for a short term let, don't waste your time looking at places that are only available for longer.

  • Whole place or flatshare? A flatshare is when you rent a bedroom and share the rest of the house with other flatsharers. Or you could rent a whole place with some friends.

  • Furnished or unfurnished? Unfurnished places might be cheaper but buying furniture may make it cost more overall.

  • Through a letting agent or directly with the landlord? You don't have to go through a letting agent. Many landlords prefer to rent directly (try OpenRent or SpareRoom).



Where to start looking


  • SpareRoom is a good place to look. You can search for short term, agent-only or use flatmate finder!

  • OpenRent links landlords and tenants directly which reduces fees!

  • rentsquare to find places with lower rent and lower fees.

  • RightMove is great for longer term rentals. Listings are from agents who may charge high fees.

  • PrimeMove is similar to RightMove.

  • Also try Gumtree or websites (and shop windows) of letting agents. Hover over the Letting Agents box for a few pointers!
Letting Agents


  • Letting agents can be helpful as they can ensure your tenancy is lawful and your bond is secure. They can act between you and the landlord and set up multiple views at convenient times.

  • The larger ones (such as Foxtons, Black Katz) probably charge higher fees but are more like to have smooth procedures

  • Remember to always ask about all the fees associated with the tenancy including agent fees, reference fees, application fees, lease termination fees, tenancy change fees, early check out fees, inventory fees, late fees and any other fees!

  • Check out allAgents for reviews of letting agents



Things to ask at viewings

  • The amount of rent (weekly or monthly)

  • The amount of deposit

  • All fees associated with the property

  • Council tax

  • Approximate cost of bills per month

  • Are any bills included in the rent?

  • How to make an application

  • Why are the previous tenants moving?

  • What date is it available?

  • How long is the tenancy?

  • What are the other tenants like?

  • Where's the nearest tube station/bus stop?

  • Where's the nearest supermarket?


What to check at viewings

  • Do the windows close properly?

  • Is there enough storage?

  • Is there any damp/mould?

  • How noisy is it? Watch for bars, trains and busy roads.

  • Is the area safe and is the place secure?

  • Do the appliances work?

  • Does it come furnished?

  • Is it too cold or hot?

  • Water pressure

  • Are there any signs of vermin?
